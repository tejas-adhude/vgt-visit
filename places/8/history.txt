National War Museum in Pune is a war memorial dedicated to post-Independence war martyrs. It was built in 1997 by the citizens of Pune who formed a war memorial fund to keep it financially stable. It was inaugurated and opened to the public on August 15, 1998, at Ghorpadi.The museum is situated in Pune Cantonment, opposite Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj Stadium on Veer Santaji Ghorpade Road. It is also known as the Southern Command National War Memorial. The museum consists of a 25 feet tall pillar made of granite that represents the sacrifices of the soldiers. The pillar is surrounded by a Mig 23 BN Jet Fighter, a T-55 Tank and a replica of INS Trishul. The museum also displays photographs, paintings, murals and artefacts related to various wars and operations that India participated in.The museum is a place of pride and honour for the Indian army and the nation. It also aims to inspire and educate the visitors about the history and achievements of the Indian defence forces. The museum is open from 9:00 am to 5:30 pm on all days of the week. There is no entry fee for visiting the museum.

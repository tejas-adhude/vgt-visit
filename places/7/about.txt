Torna Fort, also known as Prachandagad, is a large fort located in Pune district, in the Indian state of Maharashtra. It was built in the 13th century by the Shiva Panth, the followers of the Hindu deity Shiva1. It was conquered by Chhatrapati Shivaji during the 17th century and became the center of the Maratha Empire12. The hill has an elevation of 1,403 metres (4,603 ft) above sea level, making it the highest hill-fort in the district.
